Overview 
TRIKON 3.0 – A Tech Triathlon is an innovation-driven hackathon designed to empower student developers, ideators, and designers to build impactful solutions using Artificial Intelligence. Building on the success of TRIKON 1.0 and TRIKON 2.0, the event continues to encourage creativity, collaboration, and real-world problem solving within the student tech community.
Centered around the theme “Build with AI for a Smarter Tomorrow”, TRIKON 3.0 is a 28-hour offline hackathon where participants develop solutions in which AI or Machine Learning serves as the core component. The event challenges teams to apply their technical knowledge, creativity, and teamwork skills while working under time constraints.

Objectives
• Promote AI-driven innovation by encouraging participants to develop impactful solutions for real-world challenges, inspired by the vision of the AI Impact Summit 2026.
• Empower Women in Tech by creating an inclusive platform and recognizing outstanding participation and achievements by female innovators.
• Encourage problem-solving and collaboration among students through a competitive hackathon environment that blends ideation, development, and pitching.

Rounds
• Round 1 – AI Vision Blueprint: The event begins with an introductory session and problem briefing. Teams will brainstorm ideas, define the problem statement, and present a structured blueprint of their solution, including the proposed AI integration.
• Round 2 – Build Sprint Alpha: Qualified teams will enter an intensive build phase to transform their ideas into working prototypes, focusing on development, AI integration, and functional implementation.
• Round 3 – Impact Arena: In the final stage, teams will present and demonstrate their solutions to the judging panel, explaining their approach, technology stack, and the impact of their AI-powered solution.
Adherence to Standards
Participants must demonstrate continuous progress throughout the hackathon. Any violation of rules or lack of meaningful progress may lead to disqualification.
Finally, the top-performing teams will be evaluated by the judging panel based on innovation, technical execution, and overall impact to determine the winner of TRIKON 3.0.

Rules
• Each team must consist of 3–5 members.
• Team members may belong to different branches, years, or colleges.
• No team member changes are allowed after registration.
• Participants must be currently enrolled in a college and must carry valid college ID cards.
• Teams must submit a one-page problem statement document during registration.
• All development must take place within the official 28-hour hackathon duration.
• Plagiarism, code copying, or reuse of existing projects without disclosure is strictly prohibited.
• Use of AI tools, APIs, frameworks, and pre-trained models is permitted, but their usage must be clearly documented and explained.
• Teams must demonstrate continuous progress throughout the hackathon.
• Participants must maintain professional conduct and teamwork during the event.
• Any violation of rules may lead to immediate disqualification. 


Elimination:
• Teams will be disqualified if they: 
• Are found guilty of plagiarism or unfair practices 
• Violate event rules or code of conduct 
• Miss mandatory presentations or demonstrations

Judging Criteria:
• Innovation and originality of the idea
• Clarity of problem statement and relevance
• Depth and correctness of AI/ML integration
• Working prototype quality and technical execution
• Scalability, usability, and real-world impact
• Team presentation and ability to answer questions

Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A)

Q1. Who can participate in TRIKON 3.0?
A. Any currently enrolled college student with a valid college ID card can participate.

Q2. What is the team size requirement?
A. Each team must have 3 to 5 members.

Q3. Can team members be from different branches or colleges?
A. Yes. Cross-branch and cross-college teams are allowed.

Q4. Can we change team members after registration?
A. No. Team member changes are not allowed after registration.

Q5. Is the event online or offline?
A. TRIKON 3.0 is an offline hackathon at MIET, Meerut.

Q6. What is the hackathon duration?
A. It is a 28-hour hackathon running from 4 April 2026 (10:00 AM) to 5 April 2026 (2:00 PM).

Q7. Is AI/ML mandatory in the project?
A. Yes. AI/ML must be the core component of your solution.

Q8. Can we use pre-trained models, APIs, and AI tools?
A. Yes, they are allowed, but usage must be clearly documented and explained.

Q9. Can we use an old project or reused code?
A. Reuse without disclosure is prohibited. Plagiarism or unfair practices can lead to disqualification.

Q10. What do we need to submit during registration?
A. Teams must submit a one-page problem statement document.

Q11. How many teams move forward after Round 1?
A. Top 25 teams are selected after the AI Vision Blueprint evaluation.

Q12. How many teams present in the final round?
A. Top 10 teams present in the Impact Arena final presentations.

Q13. What happens if a team misses mandatory demos or presentations?
A. Missing mandatory checkpoints may result in disqualification.

Q14. What are judges looking for most?
A. Judges evaluate innovation, technical execution, AI integration, and overall real-world impact.

Q15. Is continuous progress required throughout the event?
A. Yes. Teams must show meaningful progress during the full hackathon period.
